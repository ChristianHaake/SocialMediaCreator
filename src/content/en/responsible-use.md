## Before use

- State the learning objective and fictional setting clearly.
- Allow only invented profiles, names, images, and situations.
- Agree boundaries for language, conflict, and sensitive topics.
- Discuss privacy, personality rights, and copyright.
- Decide whether results remain in class or may be shown elsewhere.

## During use

- Supervise tasks and group processes.
- Do not allow simulations of real students or educators.
- Stop demeaning, deceptive, or discriminatory content early.
- Require regular checks of sources, context, and intended impact.
- Use exports only for the agreed educational purpose.

## After use

- Review results together and delete misleading files.
- Discuss how simulations can appear outside their original context.
- Check sources, images, and permissions.
- Share or publish learning products only after approval.

## Reflection questions

- How can the simulation be recognized?
- Who could misunderstand or be harmed by the content?
- Which real person or group might feel targeted?
- What effect do profile images, timestamps, and metrics create?
- What information is missing for a fair assessment?
- How does the content change when shared without classroom context?

## Good practice

- Use fictional default profiles instead of real names.
- Present issues from multiple perspectives.
- Make sources and uncertainties visible.
- Provide clear ways to leave difficult scenarios.
- Share exports with their task or reflection, not in isolation.
- Distinguish simulation, interpretation, and verifiable facts at the end.
